My takeaways

The real growth lever isn't digital. It's relationships.

Your best clients come from introductions, recommendations, and repeat business. Estate agents, property search agents, developers, architects, and the occasional direct enquiry from someone who's heard the name.

That's not going to change. When someone's spending a million pounds with you, they need to trust you first. And trust comes from people, not pixels.

So rather than trying to generate enquiries from Instagram (which, as we discussed, isn't going to deliver a DM saying "can you quote on my £14 million chalet please?"), the question becomes: how do we make the introduction engine more deliberate, more consistent, and harder to ignore?

The brand needs to match the work.

You said something that stuck with me: "We want you to fall off your chair."

That tells me everything. You know the current online presence doesn't reflect the level you operate at. Sophie Patterson has nearly 700k followers. That number doesn't win you projects, but the perception of quality behind it matters. When an estate agent recommends you and the wife Googles you, what she sees needs to feel like a £33 million business, not a regional practice.

The rebrand and new website will help enormously. But the content, the cadence, the overall feel of how Laughland Jones shows up online needs to support that, not undermine it.

You need a partner, not another pair of hands.

You told me you're strategising, ideating, executing, reviewing, and analysing, all in two and a half days a week. That's five jobs. And when you try to focus on one, everything else stops.

You don't need someone to just "do the socials." You need someone who can think with you, challenge you, and then actually get the work done. That's a different kind of relationship to what most agencies offer.

The plan.

A 90-day programme in three phases, designed to build the marketing infrastructure that a business of your calibre should have.

PHASE 1
Phase 1: Foundations (Weeks 1-4)

The goal: get the strategic fundamentals right, so everything that follows has purpose and direction.

Introducer Programme Design

Your business development works. The team are out there shaking the right hands. But there's no system behind it. When an estate agent says "I'd like to recommend you," what happens next? We'll design a structured introducer programme that makes it effortless for estate agents, property search agents, architects, and developers to refer clients to you. Not a referral "scheme" - a curated experience that feels worthy of the brand.

Portfolio Piece Development

You mentioned the LJQ and the bound booklets you send out. That instinct is exactly right. We'll create a premium design portfolio piece: something with weight, texture, and substance that can be sent with flowers to a new homeowner, left with an estate agent, or handed to a prospect at an event. Not a "brochure." A piece that makes people think "this is the kind of company I want working on my home."

Brand Presence Audit

With the rebrand and new website in progress, we'll review how the new brand identity translates across every touchpoint: social media, email signatures, proposals, the LJQ documents, printed materials. Everything should feel cohesive and considered. We'll identify the gaps and create a plan to close them.

Content Strategy

You've got 200+ projects of stunning photography. That's a content library most businesses would kill for. We'll build a content calendar that uses this archive strategically: what to post, when, where, and why. Instagram gets a defined visual identity (think Fabric Design, the example I showed you). LinkedIn becomes a platform for positioning Andrew and Russell as thought leaders, which also helps with recruitment.

PHASE 2
Phase 2: Build and Launch (Weeks 4-8)

The goal: create the tangible assets and put the systems in place.

Introducer Welcome Sequence

A structured email flow for new introducers (estate agents, property search agents, architects). Not generic newsletters. Personalised, considered communication that keeps Laughland Jones front of mind when they're next recommending an interior designer. Each touchpoint should feel as bespoke as your design work.

Prospect Nurture Sequence

When someone expresses interest but doesn't immediately proceed, what happens? Currently, nothing structured. We'll build a sequence that stays in touch without being pushy: project showcases, design insights, and subtle proof points that keep you top of the list when they're ready to commit.

Instagram Transformation

We'll redesign your Instagram presence from the ground up. A defined grid aesthetic that reflects the new brand. Content pillars that give structure to what you post. Templates and guidelines so Alisha (or whoever's posting) isn't starting from scratch every time. The goal isn't followers. It's that when someone lands on your profile, it takes their breath away.

LinkedIn Positioning

Andrew and Russell should be visible on LinkedIn. Not posting every day, but present enough that when someone searches for them, they look like the leaders of a world-class design studio. Profile optimisation, a handful of well-crafted posts, and positioning that supports both client acquisition and talent attraction.

Monthly Email Programme

You've started the monthly email. Good. We'll take it further: segmented lists (B2B introducers vs. B2C prospects vs. past clients), content that's worth opening, and a design treatment that matches the new brand. Each send builds the relationship a little further.

PHASE 3
Phase 3: Refine and Accelerate (Weeks 8-12)

The goal: review what's working, optimise, and start building momentum.

Introducer Activation

The portfolio pieces are printed. The welcome sequences are live. Now we support your BD efforts with targeted outreach to specific estate agents, property search agents, and developers. This might include physical mailings (the design book with a handwritten note and flowers), coordinated with email and LinkedIn touchpoints. Not cold outreach. Warm, considered, multi-touch.

Event and PR Support

You want to be more visible at design shows, on panels, and in the press. We'll help identify the right opportunities, prepare supporting materials, and make sure every event appearance is amplified across your channels. When Andrew speaks at a design show, that content should work for months afterwards.

Performance Review

At the end of 90 days, we'll review everything together. What's landed? What needs adjusting? Where should we double down? This isn't a vanity metrics report. It's a practical assessment of whether the marketing now reflects the business, and what the next phase should look like.

What you get.

  • A systematic introducer programme - that turns word-of-mouth from something that happens by accident into something you can deliberately build. Estate agents, architects, and developers actively recommending you, supported by materials that make it easy for them to do so.
  • A brand presence that matches the work - Online, in print, in person. When someone Googles Laughland Jones, what they find should feel like a one-of-a-kind business. Because that's what it is.
  • A content system that runs without panic - No more posting something just because it's been too long since the last post. A structured calendar, defined visual identity, and content that Alisha can execute with confidence in two and a half days a week.
  • Email infrastructure that nurtures relationships - Introducers hear from you regularly. Prospects stay warm. Past clients feel valued. None of it requires you to remember to "send that email."
  • A marketing partner who understands the brief - Someone Alisha can call on to think through ideas, challenge assumptions, and then actually do the work. Strategy and execution. Not one or the other.

Your investment.

This isn't a standard digital marketing package. It's a bespoke engagement designed for a business that operates at the top of its field and needs its marketing to reflect that.

For context: if one additional project lands as a result of this work, even a modest one, the return on this investment is many multiples over. Your projects run into the hundreds of thousands. This is a rounding error against the revenue a single new client represents.

After the initial 90 days, the engagement continues month to month. No lock-in. We stay because we're delivering, not because of a contract.
