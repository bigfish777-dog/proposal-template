My read on where you are

Let's face it, you're in a fantastic position already.

  • 25 years of strategy work
  • A framework you can teach (and have already used with clients)
  • A book coming out in October with a publisher behind it and virtually guaranteed 'best seller' status
  • A pricing model that works (£9k for the year is the right number for the work you're doing).
  • A perfectionist's eye for the words, the brand, the experience.

What you don't have yet is a way for the right CEOs to find you.

That's what you described as "the machine". Kelly used the same word on the call, and she's right.

You need marketing that runs without you driving it, that builds an audience before the book lands, and that gets qualified CEOs onto a call with you.

Consistently. Week after week.

We can build that. But before I talk about how, I want to be honest about one thing that came up on the call.

The opportunity hiding in your positioning

When I looked at your website, your business-on-a-page, your brand framework, the thing that stood out was that the work is sharp but the way you're talking about it isn't quite there yet.

There's nothing wrong with what you're delivering, obviously. More that the language isn't yet refined in way that a busy CEO (who doesn't know you yet) will read once and think "fuck me, Charlie's read my mind!! I need to speak to her".

"Inspiring CEOs to identify their unique path to business momentum" is true. It's also the kind of sentence you and I would write because we're close to the work.

But it's not the kind of sentence that makes a scale-up founder stop scrolling.

Here's why this matters: a machine without a sharp message just sends the wrong words to more people, faster. We've watched it happen.

So the first thing we'd do is help you take the brilliant work you've already done and translate it into language that lands with the CEO you actually want to attract.

This isn't a strategy project. It's a sharpening exercise. We'll time-box it and crack on. Three weeks max.

What we'd build for you over the next 90 days

Three workstreams, running in parallel.

WORKSTREAM 1
Sharpen the proposition (weeks 1-3)

A strategy session with Ad to pull out the language that's currently sitting under your framework. Not to change what you do. To translate it.

We'd come out of this with:

  • A sharpened proposition you can use everywhere, written in CEO language
  • One primary archetype picked, plus one secondary we'll test against (we won't try to speak to scale-up founders, family business CEOs, and new-in-role CEOs all at once, that's where most messaging breaks)
  • Three to five core message angles to test in market
  • A positioning line for your LinkedIn, your website, your email signature, the lot

Look, you're the strategist. We're not going to teach you strategy.

What we are going to do is what every strategist needs but rarely gets, which is someone outside the work, who's done it for a hundred experts before you, holding a mirror up.

WORKSTREAM 2
Build the machine (weeks 2-8)

This is the bit you and Kelly asked for. Here's how we'd build it.

Top of funnel:

LinkedIn. Three to four posts a week, not one. We plan, draft, and schedule. You review and approve. You keep control of the words, we take away the burden of having to think about it every Monday morning. Built around the sharpened messaging from workstream one.

Lead capture:

a Strategic Essence Diagnostic. A short, useful tool (think 10-15 questions) that helps a CEO identify their own blind spots. It captures their email, gives them something genuinely valuable in return, and starts the conversation. Doubles as your email list builder now and your book pre-order list builder later. One asset, two jobs.

Nurture:

a weekly email. Gabriella drafts (you've met her, she writes for Tess Cope and a lot of our clients), you review, we send. Story-based, not lecture-based. Kelly's right that hard-nosed CEOs don't have time for soft anecdotes, so we'd adapt the format we use for Tess to fit your audience: shorter, sharper, with the insight up top. Built to land in inboxes on a Tuesday morning when CEOs are actually reading.

Conversion:

a strategy call booking page. The job of the machine in phase one isn't to sell the programme. It's to get qualified CEOs onto a 30-minute call with you. You close from there. We'll build the page, set up the tech, hook it into your Kajabi setup (Kelly's already done fantastic work there, we'll work alongside what she's built rather than rebuild it).

WORKSTREAM 3
The direct play (weeks 1-8, running alongside)

The machine takes time to warm up. Your goal of clients before October doesn't.

So we run a second track. A tight, deliberate, high-quality outreach campaign to a hand-picked list. The magnifying glass idea you've had is the right instinct, we just want to build it into a proper sequence instead of a one-off.

Here's what that looks like:

  • Researched list of 20-30 CEOs who fit the sharpened ICP from workstream one
  • A physical mailer (yes, the magnifying glass, plus a properly written letter from you)
  • LinkedIn outreach to the same list, sequenced after the mailer lands
  • Follow-up sequence we manage end-to-end

This is the bit that pays for the engagement in the near term. The machine pays for it from October onwards.

Setting the target honestly

You said five to fifteen clients before October. I want to set this honestly, the way we set targets for ourselves and our clients. We call it Win, Wow, Whoa.

  • Win: 3 clients. This is realistic from the direct play alone if your warm-to-lukewarm ties come good.
  • Wow: 8 clients. This is the direct play landing well, plus the machine starting to convert from week 6 onwards. Strong outcome.
  • Whoa: 15 clients. This is everything firing. Direct play exceeds expectations, 'machine' starts converting earlier than expected, and you're saying "yes" to introductions you're currently uncomfortable with.

The 15 number isn't impossible. It just needs a lot of things to break right, and one of them is you. Not the marketing.

What we're not doing in phase 1

A few things that might surprise you given they're often on the list:

  • The book funnel. This belongs in phase 2, from around week 6 onwards, in time to drive pre-orders into October. Building it now would be too early.
  • Paid LinkedIn ads at any real spend. A small test budget (£500-£1k) might go in late in phase one once we know the messaging works. Not before. Spending on ads with messaging that hasn't been tested is how money gets wasted.
  • Instagram, Facebook, podcast, YouTube. Wrong channels for your audience. Distraction.
  • A rebuild of the website or Kajabi. Kelly's already on this, and we'd be duplicating work. We'll plug into what's there.
  • Proposal templates, sales decks, brochures. You're a strategist. You can write a proposal. The gap is reach and resonance, not packaging.

I won't lay it out in detail because the right phase 2 depends on what we learn in phase 1. But the shape of it:

  • Scaling the parts of the machine that are working, killing the parts that aren't
  • Direct outreach to a larger list, informed by what landed in phase 1

One thing on how we'd work

You said something on the call I want to address head on. You're a perfectionist about your words and you're scared of letting anyone else write them. Fair enough. Most experts feel the same when they first work with us. Here's how we handle it:

You get one review pass on every piece of copy. We draft, you mark up, we revise and send. Not three passes, not five. One. That keeps your voice on the page and keeps the machine moving. If something's wrong, we fix it. If it's just a different word choice, we move on. Charlie, this matters because if you can't operate in that mode, we'll know in week two, and we'll either adjust or part ways amicably. Better to surface it now than three months in.

We don't use AI to write client copy. Gabriella writes the emails. Ad and the team work on the strategy and creative. You'll know who's drafting what.
