Part 4

Why We'd Do It Differently

On the quiz with ad spend

  • A £15M founder filling in a quiz because they saw a Facebook ad isn't how this audience behaves
  • Positioning risk: CC UK's appeal is exclusivity and curation — a paid traffic funnel sends the wrong signal
  • The addressable market is ~15,000–25,000 businesses. A well-run referral programme gets you further for almost nothing

On mass marketing

  • This audience doesn't join things because of ads. They join because someone they respect said it was worth their time
  • Without consistent internal resource, it stalls before it generates results
We're not saying ignore brand awareness entirely. We're saying targeted visibility in the right places is a different thing.
Part 5

The Better Play

Create more moments where the right people get close enough to experience the room. The product sells itself once someone's in it.

Recommendation 1: A highly qualified book-a-call funnel

A short application before someone can book a call with a director. The qualification process itself signals exclusivity. The questions should feel like a thoughtful filter, not a form. The call needs to feel like a peer conversation, not a sales call.

Recommendation 2: A request-a-brochure funnel

A physical brochure does something a PDF never can — it sits on a desk, gets picked up again, gets passed on. Qualify before sending. Only spend on people worth spending on. Clear next step inside: book a call, visit a chapter, speak to a director.

You can do anything, but you can't do everything. The conversation we need to have is which of these moves the needle fastest with the resource you've actually got.
Also worth considering

The Wider Menu

  • Post-event follow-up — a structured 30-day personal sequence from the director for every attendee who doesn't join on the day
  • Introducer programme — accountants, lawyers and commercial finance brokers talking to your ideal member every week. Give them a reason and a toolkit to refer
  • Targeted brand awareness — directors posting consistently on LinkedIn, member stories in the right networks. Not broad reach. Visible in the right places
  • Member referral process — members are already referring people without any tools. Give them something easy to forward and a clear next step to point people to
