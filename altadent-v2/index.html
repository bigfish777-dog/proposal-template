My takeaways from our conversation

Sim, thanks for your time last week. I genuinely enjoyed hearing about what you've built. The Treatment Explainer tool impressed me, and the fact that you've tested and proven it inside your brother's clinic before taking it to market puts you ahead of 90% of the SaaS founders I talk to.

But I'm going to be straight with you, because I think that's more useful than telling you what you want to hear.

You asked me for a website redesign, an Instagram setup, some carousels, and a content strategy. We could do all of that. But I don't think that's where the value is.

Here's what I heard on our call:

You've got a product that works. 25% uplift in case acceptance. £8,10k per month in additional revenue for your clinics. That's a no-brainer for any practice owner once they understand it.

You convert well when you get in front of people. Your own words: people get on a call with you and say "Sim, this is 10 times better than the big players, why are you not out there?"

The window is closing. You said competitors are coming. The tech will be replicable. The API relationships with finance providers give you a head start, but head starts don't last forever.

So the question isn't "what does your Instagram look like?" The question is: how do you get in front of 30 to 40 dental clinics in the next six months, before someone else does?

The answer is a combination of getting your foundations right quickly, and then pressing hard on paid traffic to fill your calendar. Both matter. But the foundations piece is a sprint, not a six-month project.

What we'd actually build

If we worked together, I'd want to focus on one thing: getting you in front of more dental clinic owners, faster.

Everything would serve that goal. Nothing else.

PHASE 1
Foundations (Weeks 1 to 6)

This is the setup. It's where we fix the messaging, build the funnel, and get your Instagram looking credible. The goal is to move quickly so that when we turn on paid traffic, everything is ready.

Messaging and positioning

Right now, the Altadent website doesn't reflect what you actually do. You said that yourself on the call. So we'd nail the messaging first, leading with case acceptance, not "AI diagnostics" or "bespoke SaaS." Those are features. The outcome is: your patients say yes more often, and you make more money from the patients you already have.

That 25% uplift number is your headline. Every dentist knows what their acceptance rate looks like. When they hear that number, they'll do the maths themselves.

Perspective funnel

We'd build a standalone landing page on Perspective. One page. Razor sharp. Designed to do one job: get a dental clinic owner to book a demo call with you.

This page works independently of your main website. It's where all traffic points, whether that's from your Instagram bio, direct outreach, or eventually paid ads. It plugs into your internal CRM so every lead is tracked and followed up automatically.

We'd also build the email follow-up sequence so that if someone lands on the page and doesn't book immediately, they're not lost. Automated nudges, case study emails, the kind of stuff that keeps you front of mind without you having to chase.

Demo call scripting

You're clearly good at closing when you get on a call, but having a structured framework means you can start thinking about scale, not just doing it all yourself. We'd script the demo call flow: the opener, the key questions, how to position the 25% uplift, how to handle objections, and how to close.

Instagram setup (12 to 18 posts)

We'd design and create the initial grid. A mix of:

  • Visual posts showing the Treatment Explainer software in action (screen recordings, before/after dashboards)
  • Carousel posts explaining the case acceptance problem and how Altadent solves it
  • Testimonial content from existing clinics
  • Short-form video content (shot by your videographer, with briefs and creative direction from us)

The goal isn't to build a content empire. It's to create a trust floor. When someone finds you, whether through an ad, a referral, or a cold outreach, and they check your Instagram (because some will), they see a credible, active page. Not three followers and zero posts.

We'd also put together a content strategy and posting schedule you can follow going forward. What to post, how often, what format works for dental clinic owners on Instagram. So once the initial 12 to 18 posts are live, you've got a playbook to keep it going, either yourself or with your videographer.

Videographer briefs

You mentioned you've got a videographer who'll be filming at clinics and can shoot content for Altadent as part of that arrangement. We'd write the briefs: what to film, what angles to capture, what testimonial questions to ask, what B-roll you need. So when they're on-site, they know exactly what to get for your content.

Deliverables from Phase 1:

  • Messaging framework and positioning document
  • Perspective landing page with CRM integration
  • Automated email follow-up sequence
  • Demo call script
  • 12 to 18 Instagram posts (designed, written, posted)
  • Content strategy and posting schedule
  • Videographer shot list and creative briefs
PHASE 2
Paid Traffic (When you're ready)

Once the foundations are in place and you've got content on the grid, we move to paid ads. This is where the real acceleration happens.

Meta ads targeting dental clinic owners and practice managers. The budget doesn't need to be huge. £300 to 600 per month to start, enough to test messaging, build data, and start filling your calendar.

Paid ads let you test messaging in days, not months. If a hook doesn't work, you know by Thursday. If it does, you scale it by Friday. And as a side benefit, running ads will build your follower count faster than organic posting ever will.

We'd handle the creative, the copy, the targeting, and the ongoing optimisation. Weekly reporting so you can see exactly what's working.

This phase doesn't need to kick in on a specific date. When you feel the foundations are solid and you're ready to press go, we press go. But I'd encourage you not to wait too long. Every week without paid traffic is a week your competitors could be using to catch up.

Deliverables from Phase 2:

  • Meta ad campaign (creative, copy, targeting, optimisation)
  • Ongoing campaign management and reporting
  • Landing page A/B testing and optimisation
  • Monthly strategy calls
PHASE 3
Optimise and Scale

By this point, we'll have real data. What messaging converts, what doesn't. Which type of clinic responds, which doesn't. We use that to sharpen everything: the ads, the landing page, the follow-up sequence.

This is also where we start layering in the personalised outreach idea I mentioned on the first call. Building prototype dashboards with prospects' own logos, sending targeted Loom videos. Your close rate is already high. If we can get the right people looking at the right thing before you even speak to them, the calls become a formality.

Deliverables from Phase 3:

  • Campaign optimisation based on real data
  • Personalised outreach templates
  • Performance review and forward planning

What we're not doing (and why)

Full website redesign. Not yet. Your website needs work, but it's not the bottleneck right now. The Perspective landing page does the heavy lifting. Website redesign is a later conversation once you've got revenue and data to inform what it should say.

Ongoing social media management. We'll set the grid up, give you the strategy, and write the briefs. But we're not going to be your day-to-day social media manager. That's not our strength, and honestly it's not what moves the needle for a business like yours.

Videography. You've got your own videographer. Great. We'll tell them what to shoot. We'll help you make the most of the footage. But we're not a production company.

LinkedIn strategy. Good channel for dental corporates longer term. Not the priority for the first 90 days. Instagram is where independent dentists are. Let's nail that first.

Multiple platforms. Instagram first. Once it's working, we can look at how content gets repurposed across YouTube Shorts, TikTok, or LinkedIn. But not yet.

This isn't us being lazy. It's us being disciplined. Every hour spent on things that don't directly lead to demo calls is an hour wasted while your competitors catch up.

For context

You've got 10 clinics on the Treatment Explainer at £275 per month. That's £2,750 in monthly recurring revenue.

If we help you add 10 more clinics in the first 90 days, and based on your conversion rate when you get in front of people that's realistic, that's an additional £2,750 per month. The retainer pays for itself before the end of the initial commitment.

And that's just the low-ticket product. Every one of those clinics is a candidate for your full-package upsell. If even three or four of those new clinics upgrade over the following six months, the ROI on this investment isn't even close.

Why us (and why not someone else)

I was transparent on the call: we're not social media experts. We're not videographers. We're not going to make you go viral.

What we are is a small team that embeds with your business, builds the strategy, and does the work. Not a plan in a PDF. The actual execution. Every week.

We've worked with B2B service businesses, SaaS companies, and niche businesses where getting in front of the right buyer is the entire game. That's your situation. The product is proven. The sales ability is there. The gap is distribution, and that's what we'd fix.

You mentioned on the call that you've seen agencies come and go with your brother's clinic. Beautiful strategy documents, then they disappear. We don't do that. We're in it with you, weekly. WhatsApp access, same-day responses, monthly reviews. If it's not working, we'll tell you before you tell us.

Next steps

I realise this is a big decision, especially when you're exploring a few options. So take the time you need.

But I'd gently point out: the setup work in Phase 1 is designed to be done fast. If we kick off in April, your foundations are in place by mid-May, and you're in a position to turn on paid traffic before the summer. That matters, because your competitors aren't waiting.
