My takeaways

Hey Andrey,

Thanks for the chat the other day. A few things stood out, and I want to set them out here before we get into the plan, because the plan only makes sense if I've understood you properly.

You've already made the strategic call

You've stepped away from commercial. You know exactly the work you want to do (high-end residential, design and build, turnkey), exactly where you want it (Midlands, not London, because London's already covered), and roughly the type of person who buys it (younger, smart-money clients building forever homes or doing serious investment refurbs).

That's a clear position. Most of the businesses we speak to spend the first six months trying to work that out. You've already done it. So we're not starting from scratch on strategy, we're starting on execution.

The current channel is referrals, and that's both a strength and a ceiling

Word of mouth has built the business to where it is. It's also, in your words, "a bit of a gambler". Some months busy, some months not. That's the nature of relying purely on referrals: the pipeline only refills when someone else decides to talk about you.

The goal isn't to replace referrals. It's to add a second source of pipeline that's a little more predictable, so the quiet months get quieter less often.

You don't want a blog and you don't want time wasters

This came up more than once, so I want to flag that I've heard it. You're not interested in chasing views, building a following, or being a "content creator". You want qualified leads from people who are ready to spend half a million plus, and you want to filter the rest out before they ever land in your inbox.

That shapes everything that follows. We're not going to recommend a content treadmill. We're going to recommend the smallest, most filtered top-of-funnel that gets the right people in front of you.

You're sitting on a credibility asset most builders would kill for

Thirteen restaurants in Birmingham, back to back, plus an SBID award. Anyone reading this in the market for a serious refurb can literally walk into Culture, Maya, Sila, Boogie Toys, or Dragonfly Cafe and look at the work. That's a level of proof most residential design and build companies can't get near.

It needs to be central to the marketing, not buried at the bottom of an "about" page.

What we'd build together

The 90-day plan focuses on three things, in this order:

  1. 1
    Setting up a qualified-leads funnel so the right kind of prospect can find you, see what you do, and self-qualify before they get on a call with you.
  2. 2
    Engineering more referrals out of the existing network and past clients, because that's the highest-trust channel you've got.
  3. 3
    Putting you in front of the right Midlands audience through targeted print, where your buyers actually are.

We're going to leave the broader social media question (Instagram, LinkedIn) until phase two, once the qualified-leads piece is live. There's a reason for that: doing those properly takes time and budget, and they tend to amplify whatever's already working. So we'll get something working first, then turn the volume up.

PHASE 1
Foundations (Weeks 1 to 4)

The goal: build the funnel and assets that everything else points at.

  • Half-day strategy session in person at our office. We'll lock down your ideal client profile in detail, the messaging that resonates with them, the offer structure, and the qualifying criteria. By the end of the session you'll have a written brief we can both work from.
  • Lead-capture page built. A dedicated page that's not your main website. Its only job is to convert a stranger who's interested in a major refurb into a qualified enquiry. We'll write the copy, design the page, and host it.
  • Qualification logic. A short application form before anyone gets to your inbox, designed to weed out the time wasters. Budget, postcode, project type, timeline. If they don't meet the criteria, they don't get through. If they do, you get a tidy summary in your inbox with everything you need to know before the first call.
  • Restaurant credibility asset. A single, well-designed document (PDF and link) that walks a prospect through the SBID-award-winning restaurant projects with photography, the businesses they were built for, and the standard you deliver to. This becomes the central proof piece across the whole campaign. It's what makes a high-net-worth homeowner take you seriously inside thirty seconds.
PHASE 2
Activate (Weeks 4 to 8)

The goal: start filling the funnel.

  • Meta ad campaign live. We build, write, and run a tightly geo-targeted Meta campaign. Targeting is Midlands postcodes only, audience filtered for income and homeowner signals, creative built around the credibility piece above. The objective is qualified application, not clicks or views.
  • Past-client and referral reactivation. We pull together the list of past clients (the ten or so projects in Knowle, Barnt Green, Dorridge, Solihull, Henley-in-Arden, Cheltenham), and write a small sequence of personal outreach pieces that goes to them and the architects, surveyors, and agents around those projects. Not a newsletter. A direct, one-to-one approach designed to surface the next referral.
  • First print ad placed. We identify the right Midlands lifestyle title (think the regional equivalent of Cheshire Life or Cotswold Life, plus possibly one architecture or interiors title), design the ad, write the copy, and book the placement.
PHASE 3
Optimise (Weeks 8 to 12)

The goal: figure out what's working, kill what isn't, scale what is.

  • Funnel performance review. Cost per qualified application, quality of conversations, conversion to project. We're looking for the channel and the message that's bringing you the right people, then concentrating budget there.
  • Second print placement booked. Based on what we've learned, either repeat the first title or test a second one.
  • Referral system formalised. If past-client outreach surfaces anything, we systematise the approach so it keeps running without your input.
  • Plan for next 90 days agreed. By the end of week 12 we'll know enough to decide whether to introduce LinkedIn, Instagram, or another channel, and to size up the ad budget if the funnel's working.

What this looks like by the end of 90 days

  • A live, working funnel that's actively producing qualified applications.
  • A credibility asset you'd be proud to send anyone.
  • A reactivation campaign that's already started conversations with past clients and your professional network.
  • A first print placement out in market, with a second booked.
  • Clean numbers on which channel is producing your best prospects, so the next 90 days can lean into it.

What we're explicitly not promising is ten new projects in twelve months. Your capacity is ten projects on top of what you're already doing, and getting there from a standing start in a market this small and this high-value would be optimistic. What we are promising is the system, the assets, and the channel work that gets you measurably closer than referrals alone ever could.

A note on what we're not doing

A few things we're deliberately not putting in this plan, because they came up on the call and I want to be straight about why:

  • No content treadmill. No daily posts, no blog. You don't want it, your audience doesn't need it, and it would cost you time and us focus.
  • No networking, events, or open houses. You told me you're not a people person and you don't want to spend time around time wasters. Noted. The plan keeps you in the office and on calls only with prospects who've already qualified themselves.
  • No LinkedIn or Instagram management in phase one. We'll review whether to add these in phase two, once we know what's working. Doing them properly is expensive and time-consuming, and we'd rather earn the right to suggest them.

We have three tiers of service. You don't need our top-tier RevOps package (that's for businesses already spending heavily on paid traffic and wanting a full marketing department). And the entry-level Copy and Consult tier doesn't include the campaign work you need.

The right fit is our Fractional Marketing Department package:

You get access to me and Ad directly, plus our team, on WhatsApp.

Ad spend (paid separately, direct to platforms)

Meta ad spend is paid by you directly to Meta, so it isn't part of our fee. For a campaign of this nature (highly targeted, premium audience, qualified-application objective), we'd recommend starting with a modest test budget for the first few weeks, then scaling based on what we learn. We'll agree the exact figure together before anything goes live, and you'll never be surprised by a number. Same applies to print placements: the publication invoices you directly.

Practically, given the project values you're working with, even one signed client from this campaign covers the entire 12 months of our fees several times over.

Why us

We've spent over a decade running marketing for expert businesses. Between Ad and me, we've generated more than £36 million in client revenue across coaches, consultants, and service businesses where the buyer's decision is high-trust and considered (which is exactly what a £500k refurb is).

We're not an agency that drops a strategy on your desk and disappears. We do the work. We write the copy, we build the pages, we run the ads, we book the placements. You stay focused on running projects.

We also work with a small number of clients at a time. You won't be account number 247. You'll have my WhatsApp number, and Ad's, and questions get answered the same day.

The honest truth: residential design and build isn't a sector we've worked in twenty times over. But the underlying problem (a high-trust, high-value, considered purchase where the buyer needs to feel confident before they'll even pick up the phone) is exactly what we're built for. That's the same problem a £30k coaching engagement has, or a £100k consulting retainer, or a half-million-pound refurb. The mechanics of building trust at a distance don't change much.

Next steps

I'll send this over and give you a few days with it. Let's get a call in the diary later this week so we can walk through anything that needs clarifying, adjust the approach if any of it doesn't land, and (if it feels right) get moving.

If anything in here doesn't ring true, or you want a different emphasis, say so on the call. This isn't fixed.

If it does ring true, the next step is straightforward: contract, kick-off date, and we'd block out a half-day for the strategy session in the first two weeks.

Either way, thanks for the conversation. Speak soon.
