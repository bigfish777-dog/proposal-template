Opening Message

David,

Good to speak yesterday. Short call, but I got what I needed.

You've built something genuinely impressive. 30+ years in salon tech, 2,000+ salons on the platform, £5m+ in revenue, a product that works, customers that stick. Most people we speak to are trying to get to where you already are.

But here's the thing. You know something's off. You said it yourself: the marketing makes you cringe.

So let me tell you what I took away from our conversation.

My takeaways

Problem #1: You've got a £5m product with a £50k marketing engine.

SalonIQ is genuinely good software. The AI receptionist is a smart move. The case studies are real. The numbers stack up. But the way it's all being presented to the world doesn't match the quality of what's behind it.

You described your own social content as "diabolical" and "embarrassing". You've got an ad running for a podcast that you can't even click through to. Happy Easter bunnies filling up screen time. Meanwhile, your competitors are out there telling better stories about worse products. That's the bit that should sting.

The product isn't the problem. The packaging is.

Problem #2: You've got no controllable way to generate demos.

You're doing about 25 installs a month. Roughly 10 from the website, 10 from SDRs, and the rest from referrals and existing clients expanding. It works. But it's not scalable, and you can't turn a dial to make more of it happen.

You said you want to double your demos. The maths is simple enough: double the leads, double the installs. But right now there's no mechanism to do that. You're running three ads with no Calendly link, no clear call to action, no funnel designed to convert a cold prospect into a booked demo. The infrastructure for growth just isn't there.

Problem #3: The clock is ticking.

You shelved the exit because the market's nervous about SaaS valuations and AI disruption. Fair enough. But you've given yourself 18 to 24 months to rebuild and go back to market.

That's not a long time.

Every month the marketing stays as it is, that's a month of growth left on the table. Not just in revenue, but in the metrics that buyers actually care about: new customer acquisition rate, demo volume, marketing ROI, brand presence. The stuff that makes a business look like it's accelerating, not coasting.

If you want to go to market in 18 months looking like a business on the up, the marketing needs to start changing now.

What we'd build together

Here's how we'd approach the first 90 days:

Phase 1: Audit, Strategy & Setup (Weeks 1-3)

The goal: Get under the bonnet, understand what's working, what isn't, and build the plan.

Kick-off strategy session (half-day, in person at our office in Balsall Common or yours in Tunbridge Wells, your call). We'll dig into your current marketing setup, your ad accounts, your demo booking flow, your content, your email. Everything gets reviewed.

Meta ads audit. We'll pull apart what you're currently running, look at the data, and identify what's worth keeping and what needs replacing. Three ads with no click-through to a podcast isn't going to cut it.

Demo booking flow review. You mentioned customers have to do all the work to book a demo. We'll map the current journey and identify where prospects are dropping off. The goal isn't to rebuild from scratch, it's to remove friction and make it easy.

Social content audit. We'll review what your team's been posting across all channels, benchmark it against your competitors, and identify the gaps. This feeds directly into the content strategy and templates we'll build in Phase 2.

Messaging and positioning review. "Salon software built by salon owners for salon owners" is decent, but it's not doing enough heavy lifting. We'll sharpen the messaging so it lands harder, particularly for cold audiences who've never heard of you.

Deliverables from Phase 1:

  1. 1
    Full marketing audit document
  2. 2
    Revised messaging and positioning angles
  3. 3
    90-day execution plan with priorities mapped out
  4. 4
    Demo booking flow recommendations

Phase 2: Build & Launch (Weeks 3-8)

The goal: Get the new ads running, the content strategy in place, and the email working properly.

New Meta ad campaigns. We'll test new hooks and creative alongside your existing ads. Different angles, different formats, different audiences. All driving to a clear "book a demo" action. We're not replacing what's working, we're testing what could work better and scaling the winners.

Social content strategy, templates and calendar. We'll build a content framework your team can execute from. This isn't us writing every post. It's us giving them the strategy, the templates, the calendar, and the playbook so the output actually matches the quality of the product. No more Easter bunnies.

Email campaigns. We'll write email sequences for your prospect list and your existing customer base. Campaigns designed to drive demos from cold leads and upsell opportunities from current clients. You send them through HubSpot, we write the copy.

Ad creative production. We'll produce the creative assets for the Meta campaigns. Video scripts, static designs, carousel concepts. Tested against each other to find what resonates with salon owners scrolling Facebook and Instagram.

Deliverables from Phase 2:

  1. 1
    New Meta ad campaigns live and running
  2. 2
    Social content strategy document, templates and 90-day calendar
  3. 3
    Email sequences written (prospect nurture + customer upsell)
  4. 4
    Ad creative library (multiple hooks and formats for testing)

Phase 3: Optimise & Scale (Weeks 8-12)

The goal: Read the data, kill what's not working, double down on what is.

Campaign optimisation. By this point we'll have enough data to know which hooks, audiences, and creative formats are winning. We cut the losers, scale the winners, and start driving down cost per demo booked.

Content review. Check in on how the social content is landing. Are the templates working? Does the team need more support? Adjust the strategy based on what we're seeing.

Email performance review. Open rates, click rates, replies, demos booked. We'll review what's working and write the next batch.

Monthly strategy call. Review everything. What's working, what's not, what's next. Clear, honest, no waffle.

Deliverables from Phase 3:

  1. 1
    Campaign performance report with recommendations
  2. 2
    Optimised ad creative (new variations based on winning hooks)
  3. 3
    Updated email sequences
  4. 4
    Next quarter plan

What we're not doing

This is just as important as what we are doing. Being clear about scope keeps everyone honest.

We're not rebuilding your website. It works, it ranks, it generates leads. Could it be better? Probably. But that's not where the biggest gains are right now.

We're not doing SEO. You already rank number one for "salon software" and as you pointed out, people aren't searching for it anyway. SEO isn't the lever here.

We're not managing your SDR team. They're doing their thing. We're focused on giving them more leads to work with, not telling them how to make calls.

We're not taking over your social media accounts. We're building the strategy, the templates, and the calendar. Your team posts. We give them something worth posting.

We're not running your HubSpot. We'll write the emails. You send them. We don't need access to your CRM or your customer data to do what we do.

Sam's personal brand

I want to flag this as an opportunity, even though it's not a core part of this proposal.

Sam's already getting press coverage. She's in Salon Magazine, she's being positioned as an industry voice, she's got the credibility. But the LinkedIn and social presence isn't matching that. It feels edited, like press releases rather than real behind-the-scenes insight.

There's a version of this where Sam becomes the go-to voice in salon tech. Not in a wanky "thought leader" way, just telling stories about what she's seeing, what's working, what's changing. That builds trust in a way that brand content never can.

It's not in the scope for the first 90 days. But once we've got the foundation right, it's worth a conversation.

Beyond 90 days: IQ Connect campaign

The AI receptionist is a genuine differentiator. The launch webinar had phones ringing off the hook. But there's no sustained campaign behind it.

Once the core marketing infrastructure is working (ads generating demos, content that doesn't make you cringe, emails that actually drive action), the next move is a dedicated campaign around IQ Connect. Positioning it not just as a feature, but as the reason salons need to modernise. That's a phase 2 conversation, but I wanted you to know we're thinking about it.

Investment

To cover the scope we've outlined, you'd need to hire at least three people: a content strategist to fix the social and messaging (c.£2,800/month employer cost), an ad specialist to run the Meta campaigns properly (c.£3,200/month), and a copywriter for the emails and ad creative (c.£2,700/month). That's roughly £8,700 a month in salary alone, before you factor in recruitment costs, onboarding time, management overhead, tools, and the reality that none of them individually would bring strategic direction. You'd still need someone telling them what to do.

With us, you get the strategy and the execution in one place. No recruitment. No management. No six-month ramp-up while someone learns your industry.

If we can move that needle by even 30-40% in the first quarter, the maths works comfortably. And if we hit your target of doubling it, the return isn't even close.

Why us?

We don't know the salon industry like you do. We never will.

But we do know how to take a B2B business with a great product and fix the marketing that sits around it. We've done it for SaaS companies, trade manufacturers, professional services firms, and dozens of expert businesses.

We've generated over £36.2 million in client revenue. We've helped businesses go from "nobody knows we exist" to "we can't onboard fast enough". And we've done it by actually doing the work, not sending strategy decks and disappearing.

You mentioned you've been filling out forms and speaking to lots of people. I'd imagine most of them have told you what you should be doing. We'll tell you what we're going to do, and then we'll do it.

We also only work with a handful of clients at any one time. You won't be account #247. You'll have our WhatsApp numbers. Questions get answered the same day, not buried in a ticketing system.
