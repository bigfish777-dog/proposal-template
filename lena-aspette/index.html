My takeaways

Ilya,

Thanks for jumping on with me on Tuesday. Two things stood out from our conversation that I want to address straight away.

The first is what you said about the freebie test the current ads guy ran: 70 views, 0 opt-ins, and your instinct as a software engineer was that something must be broken because zero isn't a real number. You're right. That isn't an ad performance problem. That's a "something is technically wrong" problem. And the fact that nobody pushed back on it tells you everything you need to know about the level of attention your paid traffic is actually getting.

The second is the number you mentioned for current revenue, around £10k a month. I'll be honest, when I saw the size of your audience before the call, I assumed it'd be 3-4x that. Which means the gap between what your business should be doing and what it's currently doing isn't a strategy problem. It isn't a product problem. The Style with Confidence program clearly works, the lookbook converts, the email list is performing at 50% open rates, and you've built an audience most coaches and course creators would sell a kidney for.

It's a lead volume problem.

You're trying to grow a business by squeezing a 10,000-person email list. And you said it yourself near the end of the call: if you keep going like this, you'll wear that list out. You need new people coming in. Fast.

That's what this proposal is about.

The problem

You're sitting on roughly 1m followers across Instagram, TikTok, and Facebook. Your email list is 10k. That's a 1% conversion from audience to list. The benchmark I work to is at least 10%.

Said another way: there are tens of thousands of people in your audience who already like what Lena does, who would happily join the email list, and who would eventually buy. They just haven't been asked properly, or they've been asked through a broken funnel.

On top of that, every time you launch Style with Confidence, you're pulling buyers from the same finite pool. Each launch shrinks the pool of fresh prospects. The numbers will keep getting harder until you fix the inflow.

The good news is the rest of the engine works. Emails open. Launches sell. The lookbook is profitable. So if we get cold traffic landing on the email list at a sensible cost, everything downstream benefits straight away.

What we'd build together

This isn't a content engagement. It isn't a positioning project. You've got those bits handled, and frankly, your organic stands up better than most of what I see.

This is a paid traffic engagement. We're going to put a working machine in front of cold audiences, build the email list, and use the front-end products (lead magnet, tripwire, lookbook) to recoup ad spend as fast as we can.

The goal isn't to promise you a specific cost-per-lead or ROAS in month one. Anyone who does that on day one is guessing. The goal is to build the system that gives you the data to decide where to keep spending and where to stop. Once we know what's working, scaling is the easy part.

Here's how the first 90 days look.

PHASE 1
Foundation & first tests (Weeks 1-4)

The goal: Get a clean, properly tracked funnel live. Stop guessing. Start collecting real data.

  • Funnel audit & rebuild. First thing we'll do is figure out why the current lead magnet test returned zero opt-ins. Could be tracking, could be the page, could be the form, could be the targeting, could be the offer-to-audience match. We'll find it and fix it. Then we'll build a proper lead capture page (we'd recommend taking the Instagram bio link straight to a lead magnet too, while we're at it).
  • Creative production. We'll script 6-8 ad concepts designed specifically for cold paid traffic, not repurposed reels. Different hooks, different angles, different audiences. You mentioned on the call that Lena's happy to shoot more, which is perfect. We script and storyboard, Lena shoots, we edit and ship. We'll also produce static and graphic-led variants to test against video.
  • Tracking & pixel setup. Proper conversion tracking from ad click through to email list and tripwire purchase. Klaviyo integration so we can see which campaigns are driving actual buyers, not just opt-ins.
  • Initial campaign launch. We start with broad targeting (Meta's algorithm now performs better with broad than tight interest stacks, and most contractors still don't run it that way) plus a custom audience of your social engagers.
PHASE 2
Learn & double down (Weeks 4-8)

The goal: Identify what's actually working and pour fuel on it.

  • Creative iteration. By week 5-6, the data will tell us which hook, which angle, and which audience are pulling. We'll script a fresh batch of variations on the winners. Expect to be shooting roughly fortnightly during this phase.
  • Tripwire optimisation. Right now the funnel goes lead magnet -> £15 tripwire -> upsell to Style with Confidence. We'll test variants of the tripwire page to push conversion up. Every extra % is more recouped ad spend, which is more headroom to scale.
  • Email integration. We'll work with your existing weekly email rhythm to make sure new subscribers are getting nurtured properly from day one, not just dumped into the same broadcast list with everyone else. We're not taking over your emails. We're making sure cold leads warm up properly before they hit a sales sequence.
  • GoHighLevel migration (if you want it). You mentioned wanting to move off Klaviyo. Most of our clients run on GoHighLevel, so this is home turf for us. If you decide to make the move during the engagement, we can handle the migration as part of the work, including rebuilding your automations, segmentation, and any landing pages or forms that need to come across. Worth flagging the deliverability dip I mentioned on the call (we've seen 10-15% drops in reported open rates after moving off Klaviyo), but with proper Mailgun setup and a warming period, it settles down.
  • A/B testing infrastructure. You mentioned your software engineer's instinct toward proper A/B testing. We'll build the structure that lets us test cleanly rather than just spitballing creative variants.
PHASE 3
Scale & systemise (Weeks 8-12)

The goal: Find the spend ceiling and operate confidently underneath it.

  • Spend scaling. Once we have a winning combination (creative + audience + funnel), we scale spend gradually. The discipline here is not breaking what works by ramping too fast. Meta will punish you for it.
  • Front-end profitability. The aim is to get the lead magnet -> tripwire -> SWC sequence to break-even or better on cold traffic. If we achieve that, list growth becomes effectively free, which changes the economics of your whole business.
  • Audience expansion. Lookalikes, retargeting layers, and bringing TikTok in as a secondary channel once Meta is dialled in.
  • Monthly reporting & strategy reviews. Proper dashboards. Clear numbers. No bullshit.

What we're not doing

Worth being explicit about this, because it's where a lot of agency engagements go wrong:

  • We're not taking over your organic content. Lena's reels are already pulling 50k average views and you don't need our help with that. We'll script content specifically for ads, but your organic strategy stays where it is.
  • We're not rebuilding your products or your launch model. Style with Confidence works. The lookbook works. We're not here to redesign things that aren't broken.
  • We're not writing your weekly newsletter. You've already got a 50% open rate, which is excellent. We'll make sure cold leads get a proper welcome sequence so they're warm by the time they hit your broadcasts, but the weekly newsletter stays yours.
  • We're not promising specific CPLs or ROAS in month one. Anyone who does is guessing. We're promising a system that produces clean data, learns fast, and scales what works.

What this means for you

By the end of 90 days, you'll have:

  • A working paid traffic engine that's been tested across multiple hooks, audiences, and offers, with clear data on what's working and what isn't.
  • A meaningfully larger email list. Hard to put a number on this without seeing early CPLs, but on £500-£1000/month spend in month one scaling up as we identify winners, we'd be aiming to add multiple thousands of new subscribers by day 90.
  • Front-end economics that can support real spend. Once the lead magnet -> tripwire sequence is profitable on cold traffic, you can scale spend almost without limit. That's where this gets exciting.
  • A library of tested ad creative that you can keep running, and that informs every future shoot.
  • A clear view of where your money's going. Proper tracking, proper reporting, proper accountability. No more "let me get the latest results from the guy who's doing it."

Most importantly, the next time you launch Style with Confidence, you're launching to a bigger, warmer, fresher list. Which means bigger launches. Which means the high-ticket coaching offer you mentioned wanting to develop has actual demand to support it.

Investment

Ad spend is separate from the retainer and paid directly by you to Meta. We don't take a cut of ad spend. We don't mark it up. What you spend on ads is yours, and it goes straight to the platform.

Setup & onboarding (month 1 only) £1,500+VAT
Monthly retainer (from month 2) £1,250+VAT/month
Recommended ad spend (month 1) £500-1,000/month (paid to Meta)

90-day initial commitment, rolling 30 days thereafter.

On ad spend specifically: £500-1,000/month is the right starting point. Enough to generate meaningful data without putting you at risk if we need a few rounds of testing to find the winners. Once we identify what's working, we'll recommend scaling. If we get to a point where front-end is breaking even or better, the sensible thing is to spend as much as Meta will let us until that changes.

For context: if a typical Style with Confidence launch converts 1.5-2% of your list at £350 average order value, then every additional 1,000 emails added to your list during this engagement is worth £5-7k in launch revenue. The maths gets compelling fast.

Either way, you've built something real with that audience. It deserves a paid traffic engine that actually works.
