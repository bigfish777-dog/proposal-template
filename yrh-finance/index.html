PHASE 1
Phase 1: Fix the foundations

Weeks 1 to 4

The goal: get the basics working so we can actually measure what's happening.

Email reactivation

This is the quick win. We'll segment the 100,000 contacts, identify the engaged portion, and build a warm-up campaign to start getting emails flowing again through Spotler. Not blasting the whole list on day one. Smart segmentation, starting small, building sender reputation, and growing from there. The aim is a consistent weekly or fortnightly email going out to an engaged audience within the first month.

Workshop follow-up system

We'll build a proper post-workshop email sequence. Someone attends a workshop, they get a follow-up campaign that moves them toward a discovery call with Debbie. This should have been in place from the start. Every workshop attendee is a warm lead who's already given you their time. We need to capitalise on that.

Website messaging tweaks

Small but meaningful. Reordering the homepage so it leads with the problem, not the company. Tightening the ScoreApp quiz positioning (we discussed reframing it around "have you outgrown your bookkeeper?" rather than a generic financial health check). Improving the conversion path so visitors have a clear next step.

A way to book a call

We talked in the session about high-intent versus low-intent traffic. Someone searching for a fractional finance solution, someone who's just had their bookkeeper resign, someone whose accountant told them they need better MI, those people don't want to read a blog post. They want to talk to someone. We need a clear, frictionless way for those people to book a discovery call, and we need to start driving traffic to it. Email first, paid later.

Reporting setup

A simple weekly/monthly dashboard that gives you the numbers you've been asking for. Email open and click rates, website traffic and conversion, workshop attendance and follow-through, leads generated by channel. Nothing fancy. Just visibility.

PHASE 2
Phase 2: Build the machine

Weeks 5 to 8

The goal: create the consistent marketing rhythm that's been missing.

Email content strategy

Regular emails to your engaged list. Not newsletters. Story-based content that positions YRH as the obvious answer when a business owner hits that moment of "my bookkeeper can't keep up." The kind of content you described in the session, the manufacturer who didn't realise his cash was trapped in stock cycles, the business owner logging into their bank account for financial intelligence. Those stories are gold. We'll write them, you'll approve them, and they'll go out consistently.

Workshop promotion campaign

A proper campaign to fill each workshop with higher quality attendees. Targeted emails to the right segments, LinkedIn promotion, and a qualification step so you're getting business owners who are actually problem-aware, not just curious.

LinkedIn strategy

Your personal profile is the most powerful asset you're not using properly. We'll build a content plan around your expertise, the kind of insight posts and client stories that make business owners in the £1.5m to £5m range think "she gets it." We'll direct the strategy, write the content, you review and post.

Debbie's outreach refinement

Small tweaks to the emails Debbie sends when leads come through. The difference between a reply and being ignored is often just how the first message is worded. We'll help tighten those up.

PHASE 3
Phase 3: Optimise and scale

Weeks 9 to 12

The goal: double down on what's working and cut what's not.

Review everything against the numbers

By this point, we'll have enough data to see what's driving inquiries and what isn't. We'll adjust the email strategy, workshop targeting, and content themes based on real performance data.

Explore paid acquisition

If the organic engine is working, this is where we start looking at paid options. LinkedIn ads targeting your ideal sectors, potentially a reactivation of PPC if the website conversion rate supports it. But only if the numbers justify it.

Scale what converts

More of whatever is generating those discovery calls. Fewer of whatever isn't. Simple.
