Our takeaways from the session

After spending a couple of hours together, it became obvious that the bottleneck isn't the product. The membership delivers. The stories from the room are extraordinary. Your conversion rate when you get a qualified person in front of you is somewhere around seventy to eighty percent. The product isn't the problem.

The bottleneck is reach.

Right now, Corporate Connections grows through personal graft. Your graft, specifically. And it works. But personal connections have a ceiling. You said it yourself: you want to find people you've never met.

There are somewhere between fifteen and twenty five thousand founder led businesses in the UK doing £5m plus. You need about sixty more of them to hit a hundred members. That's not a mass marketing exercise, but it does need something more deliberate than organic referrals alone.

That's what this proposal addresses.

We're not here to replace what's already working. The referrals, the introductions, the personal relationships, that engine keeps running. What we're here to do is fill the gaps and amplify. You've got the sales ability. You've got a VA team coming online to handle the operational side. What you don't have is a marketing function that's putting qualified strangers on your calendar and keeping Corporate Connections front of mind with the people who aren't ready yet. That's the bit we do.

There are two distinct pieces here, and they're designed to work independently. You can say yes to one and not the other. We've separated them deliberately so there's no all or nothing decision to make.

How it works

A business owner sees the ad on Facebook or Instagram. It's short, typically under twenty seconds, direct, and immediately positions CC as something different from every networking group they've seen before.

They click through to a page with the VSL. They watch. If they're qualified and interested, they fill in a short application form and book a call with you.

You do what you already do brilliantly. Have the conversation. Build rapport. Identify opportunities. Invite them to a meeting.

We handle everything upstream of that call. Your job is to close. Our job is to fill the calendar.

The secondary benefit: awareness

Even before anyone books a call, the ads are doing work. Based on the budget we're recommending, you should expect around 15,000 impressions per month. That's 15,000 business owners seeing Corporate Connections who don't currently know you exist. Some of them won't be ready now. But when they hear the name again six months later, from a member, from an introducer, at a golf club, they'll recognise it. That brand awareness compounds over time, and you're getting it as a byproduct of the direct response campaign.

Creative approach

We start with 6 ad creatives and test them against each other. The data tells us which hooks, which messages, and which formats are resonating. Every fortnight, we produce 6 new creatives based on what the data is showing us. The ads that work get doubled down on. The ones that don't get replaced. We apply the same split testing discipline to the landing page, the form, and the email sequences.

This isn't a set and forget campaign. It's an iterative, data driven process. The worst thing to do is spend two months perfecting everything before anything goes live. We get it out, test it, and improve from there.

Targeting

We'd recommend starting with Birmingham and the surrounding area. The maths is simple: a Birmingham member is more valuable to you because you retain more of the revenue, and it strengthens your home group, which in turn improves renewals. Growing the group stops the slide and creates momentum.

Once we've got proof of concept and the numbers are stacking up, we can expand targeting to other areas.

Part 2
Content, Emails & Brand Building

This is the awareness engine. The thing that builds the Corporate Connections brand from a well kept secret into something that business owners doing £5m plus actually recognise and talk about.

You made the comparison to BNI and Entrepreneur Circle. Both have spent years building awareness. You don't have years to wait, and frankly you don't need to. You've got better stories than either of them. You're just not telling them.

What we recommend

Regardless of whether you engage us to execute this, here's what needs to happen:

  • 1. Weekly storytelling emails to the database. Not newsletters. Stories. The coffee club at Ferrari. The deal that got put together between two members who would never have met otherwise. The guy who came to one meeting and within a month had three new introductions worth more than his membership fee. These stories already exist. They just need to be told consistently, in your voice, every single week. This is the single most powerful thing you can do to keep Corporate Connections front of mind with the people who aren't ready to join yet but will be eventually.
  • 2. A proper database with qualification markers. Every guest, every visitor, every prospect needs to be captured and categorised. Qualified, aspiring, introducer, not a fit. London alone had thirty guests who never went anywhere because nobody had their details. That can't keep happening. The CRM is coming online with your VA team, and this is a great first job for them. The structure and tagging needs to be right from day one so that when you've got five hundred contacts in there, you can actually do something useful with them.
  • 3. LinkedIn presence from Pete personally. Not the Corporate Connections page. You. The personal posts carry more weight, more authenticity, and more reach. You don't need to post every day. One or two posts per week about what's happening, who you've met, what introductions were made in the room. Done tastefully. The goal is salience. When someone sees an ad for Corporate Connections and then goes to look you up, what they find needs to reinforce the message.
  • 4. Post event follow up sequences. You already call prospects within twenty four hours. That's excellent and should continue. But after that initial call, the conversation currently stops. There needs to be a system that keeps those people warm. Invitations to future events, stories from the room, reminders of what they experienced. The emotional window doesn't close after one phone call if you keep feeding it.
  • 5. Introducer enablement. Your introducers want to help. They just don't get round to it because it isn't easy enough. The collateral needs to feel natural to them. Rather than one polished pack that sits in a drawer, give them a sentence, a paragraph, and a page. Let them choose what fits their relationship with the person they're introducing. And when a member says they've got someone to refer, make it happen in the room. Help them send the email or make the call before they leave. Don't let it become something they'll get round to later.
  • 6. Event positioning and pricing perception. List the summit ticket at £995. Even if every single person comes in on a free invitation. The listed price signals value. A fifteen thousand pound membership that has a hundred and forty seven pound event attached to it creates a disconnect. You don't need to charge people. You just need the published price to match what Corporate Connections actually is. This is a simple change that shifts perception immediately.
  • 7. The quiz, through partners only. You've already built the Strategic Clarity Report. It's ready to go. Get the VA team to make it live as their first task, then test it with five partners who have databases of £5m plus businesses. Don't spend money driving traffic to it. That would dilute the intent. But distributing it through warm channels costs nothing and gives you data. If it works, scale it. If it doesn't, you've got your answer.

The funnel brings in people who are actively looking right now. The content and email work builds the next pipeline. It warms up the people who aren't ready yet. It keeps Corporate Connections front of mind so that when someone at a golf club mentions networking, the other person says "have you heard of Corporate Connections?"

It also supports the funnel directly. When someone sees an ad and goes to look you up, what they find needs to reinforce the message. Consistent content does that. Without it, you're relying entirely on the ad to do all the heavy lifting. With it, the prospect arrives on the call already feeling like they know who you are.
