My takeaways

Thanks for the time on the phone earlier in the week, Digesh. You were refreshingly honest about where things are, which makes my job easier.

Here's what I took away:

You've grown to where you are without a real marketing engine

Word of mouth, referrals, the odd bit of website traffic. It's worked, but it's lumpy. You've never had a way to deliberately turn the tap on, and that's left you exposed to the months where the referrals dry up.

You've got niches that work, but you've been afraid to commit to them

Insurance is the obvious one. The projects are bigger, the case studies are there, the model fits (lead management, case management, portals, documents). But there's a fear that if you niche the messaging too hard, you cut off the wider opportunity.

I'd push back on that, gently. The fear of niching is almost always more expensive than the niching itself. You don't lose the other work, you just stop being mediocre at attracting it.

You've tried weekly emails and they didn't work

You sent the boring kind. Facts. Info. Guides. Useful, but not the kind that builds a relationship or gets a reply. They went out, did nothing, you stopped. I hear this almost every week from prospects.

There's a version of weekly emails that actually works, but it's the opposite of what most people send. More on that below.

The website's been redeveloped, but it's not getting much traffic

We could spend time perfecting the messaging on the site. We're not going to. Not yet.

It'd be like getting a beautiful new sign made for a shopfront that's tucked away on a sidestreet nobody walks down. Until we sort the signposting, the sign doesn't matter. We'll come back to the site once we know what's actually converting from the ads, because then we'll know what message to put on it.

The opportunity

You said it yourself. Average transaction value last year was £12-13k. Insurance projects average £30k, with peaks at £50-60k. On top of that, you've got recurring revenue from license reselling, onboarding, and ongoing support.

You don't need hundreds of new clients. You need a small number of right clients, consistently.

Last year you turned over £394k with a heavily referral-led model. If we can put two or three properly-qualified insurance prospects in front of you every month, with a sales process that's built for your type of consultative selling, you're looking at a fundamentally different business this time next year.

The thing that's missing is the system that gets those prospects in front of you in the first place. That's the bit we build.

The approach

A 90-day initial engagement, then rolling 30 days. Two things running in parallel from week one.

1. Book-a-call funnel (the new pipeline)

This is the main build. The same model we use to fill our own diary, adapted for what you sell.

It looks like this: short, no-nonsense video ads on Meta (and likely LinkedIn) → simple landing page → a short VSL where you talk directly to your ideal client (the insurance firm decision-maker) → application form → booked call in your diary.

We do the lot:

  • Scripting for the ads and the VSL
  • Filming day at our studio in Balsall Common (about 90 minutes, professional kit, proper direction so you don't have to think about it)
  • Editing and ad creative production (six creatives to start, then six new every fortnight based on what's performing)
  • Landing page build, copy, and integration
  • Ad account setup and ongoing management
  • Booking system, automations, follow-up sequences
  • Sales process design so the call itself is built around your consultative style (we'll help you build the framework, you and your team run the calls)
  • Reporting

A note on you being on camera. You're a technical operator, not a marketer, and being the face of the funnel might feel uncomfortable. That's normal. Most of our clients feel the same way before the shoot day. We'll script it, direct it, and edit it so the version of you that ends up on screen is sharp, credible, and not cringing. The shoot itself is the least painful part of the whole process. Promise.

2. Weekly emails to your existing list (the dormant revenue)

You've already got a list. You've already tried emails. We're going to do them differently.

The version of weekly emails that works isn't a digest of Microsoft Dynamics updates and tips. It's short, personal, anecdotal emails written in your voice, that build a relationship with the people on your list over time. A story from a recent project. A thing a client said. An opinion on something happening in the industry. They're not selling. They're keeping you in their head so that when the moment comes, they think of you first.

For context, one of our clients, Tess, grew 40% last year to do seven figures off the back of this exact strategy. She runs a completely different kind of business to you, but the principle travels. People buy from people they feel they know. Weekly emails done properly are the cheapest, most reliable way to do that at scale.

We write them. You approve them. They go out under your name.

What we're not doing (yet)

A deliberate list, because it matters as much as what we are doing:

  • Cold outreach (LinkedIn or email). It'd work for you eventually, especially a targeted "dream 20" play to specific insurance firms with hyper-personalised follow-up. But it's expensive to deliver well, and it makes more sense once the funnel's producing data and we know exactly who's converting. Phase two conversation.
  • Rebuilding the website. Covered above. Right move, wrong time.
  • Anything for Grassroots to Glory. Different audience, different business, different funnel. There's a real opportunity there but it needs its own plan, and trying to do it alongside the main piece would dilute both. Park it. Revisit in the next quarter.

Investment

Here's how it works.

One-off: Funnel build & setup

Everything in section 1 of the approach. Funnel built, VSL filmed, page live, ads running. This is the chunk of work that gets the system stood up. £5,750+VAT

Monthly: Management & growth

Per month £1,250+VAT

Covers two things:

  • Ongoing paid traffic management, creative iteration, ad refresh every fortnight, reporting and optimisation
  • Weekly emails (written, approved, scheduled, sent) to your existing list and any new leads the funnel brings in

90-day initial commitment, then rolling 30 days.

Commission on closed clients

On any project over £15k 10% of first-year contract value

This is the piece that aligns us properly. If we don't get you the right kind of clients, we don't earn the commission. We're not interested in flooding you with £100-a-month jobs. We want the £15k+ projects, because those are the ones that move the needle for both of us.

A few specifics:

  • "First-year contract value" = the total value of the project plus the first 12 months of any recurring licence/support revenue that comes with it
  • Commission applies to any client closed during the engagement that we've contributed to. Funnel leads obviously. Also any prospect on your list that we've been nurturing through the weekly emails.
  • Six-month attribution tail. If we generated the lead during our engagement and they close within six months of the engagement ending, the commission still applies. (This protects us from a scenario where leads get parked, then close after we've finished.)
  • Invoiced monthly, payable on receipt of payment from the client

Ad spend (paid directly to Meta)

£2,000 to £3,000 per month, to start

Important to be straight about this. Ad spend is not paid to us, it's paid directly to Meta. There's no markup.

For the first 60-90 days, treat this as a learning investment, not a performance test. You're buying data on which hooks, audiences, and offers actually convert. Once we know that, it becomes the most predictable cost in your business: pay £X, get Y booked calls, close Z clients. But the first two months are exploration, not optimisation. You need to be comfortable spending the £2-3k a month knowing the worst realistic outcome is that you'll have learned a lot and not closed a client. The likelihood of that is small, but it's real, and I'd rather we both go in eyes open.

After we've found what's working, the ad spend either holds, scales up (if the maths is strong), or, in rare cases, gets paused while we rework the creative. You decide.

The maths

Worth running the numbers, because the commission piece looks bigger on the page than it is in reality.

Let's say the funnel performs modestly and we land you one qualified insurance client per month at a £30k average project value.

  • 12 clients × £30k = £360k in project revenue alone
  • Plus recurring license and support revenue, which on your numbers is a meaningful tail
  • Our commission at 10% of first-year contract value = approximately £3,000 per client (assuming £30k first-year value)
  • Annual commission to us = c.£36k
  • Plus £15k in monthly retainer = £51k total in ongoing fees
  • Plus the £5,750 setup

Total cost to you for the year: roughly £57k.

Revenue generated: £360k+ from projects alone, before recurring revenue and the compounding effect of those clients being on your books for years.

If the funnel underperforms and we land you half that, you've still added £180k in project revenue, and we earn proportionally less. The pricing only gets expensive for you if it's working. Which is rather the point.

So, why us?

A few things worth knowing.

We don't do strategy decks. We don't do "here's the plan, good luck running it." We're a fractional marketing department. We script, shoot, build, write, run ads, manage the inbox, and report on what's working. You don't manage us, we manage ourselves.

We work with a handful of clients at a time. You'd get me, Ad (my business partner), and our team in the background. Not an account manager handing things down a chain.

We've built funnels like this for ourselves and for clients. The book-a-call model isn't theoretical for us, it's how we fill our own diary. The ad you saw, the call you booked, the conversation we had earlier this week. That's the system. We're proposing we build you the same thing, tuned to your audience.

And the commission piece. We've never run it quite this way before, and I'm being upfront about that. You're the right kind of client to do it with, because you sell a high-value, repeatable product to a clear niche, and because I trust we'll build something that works. We're putting our money where our mouth is. If we don't get you the right kind of clients, we don't earn the commission. Simple as that.

Next steps

Have a read, sit on it, push back where you want to. Few things to think on:

  1. 1
    The commission piece is the most unusual part. If anything in there feels off, let's talk through it before you sign anything. I'd rather we get the structure right at the start than rework it in three months.
  2. 2
    Capacity. If this works and we're putting two or three new insurance clients in front of you every month, you need to be set up to deliver them. Worth a 15-minute conversation about what your delivery ceiling looks like and whether we need to factor that in.
  3. 3
    The ad spend commitment. You need to be properly comfortable with the £2-3k/month in months one and two, knowing it's CAPEX. If you're going to flinch at month two when we haven't closed a client yet, we need to talk about that now, not then.

Drop me a line when you've had a chance to digest. Happy to jump on another call to walk through any of it.
