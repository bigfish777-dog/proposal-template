  • You've got two VSLs that need wrangling.
  • And you want everything stable, scalable, and stress-free (understandable with the August deadline looming!)

You also said this: "I'd like the rate to reflect the work you do and not the turnover that I do or the price points that I sell".

Fair. That's how we've priced this, because we like to work with our clients for a long time, build proper relationships, and really embed with them - not have to go out and replace a client every 6 weeks.

It's not dirt cheap - because there's lots to do and pretty quickly - but it's not inflated because you're turning over a couple of mil.

So, without further ado, here's what we'll do for you...

The 90-day plan

You said the priorities are:

  1. 1
    Consistent predictable cash flow through the VSL
  2. 2
    The paid challenge launching in June (and automating it like Russell's)
  3. 3
    Ecosystem and automation

So that's how we'd structure the first 90 days. Three workstreams running in parallel, with the right people on each one, and everything aimed at having the engine humming by August.

WORKSTREAM 1
VSL cash flow (Weeks 1-4, then ongoing)

Get one VSL converting consistently. Then get the second one launched. Then test, optimise, scale.

  • Audit the current VSL (the case-study-189788 one) and rewrite the messaging to land on your actual ICP, not the quick-win/mental-health audience it's currently chasing
  • Build a new funnel for the new VSL you've just shot
  • Set both up properly in GHL, with proper tracking, follow-up sequences, and a rebooking flow for no-shows
  • Run paid traffic into both, A/B test, scale what works

By the end of week 4 you should have at least one VSL converting predictably. By week 8 you've got two, and we know which one to scale.

WORKSTREAM 2
The June paid challenge (Weeks 1-5)

You've got the swipe files. We've got the team. We build it.

  • Full funnel build: free opt-in → £17 bump → £37 bump → £97-£147 VIP
  • Email sequence across the welcome party, three challenge days, secret day 4, doors close, encore webinar
  • Daily ads through the lead-in and during the challenge
  • Pitch and re-pitch creatives
  • Post-challenge instant wins amplification
  • 24-hour encore window setup

Goal: be in profit before doors close. Same way you've done it before when you ran your own ads.

Then we run it again in July, refined. Lovely.

WORKSTREAM 3
Email reactivation and the ecosystem (Months 2-3)

The 170k list isn't dead. It's dormant. Which is good news, because we've resuscitated more email lists than a good vet has hamsters.

  • Audit, segment, scrub, re-engage
  • Get a proper broadcast cadence in place
  • Build evergreen automations across the four facilitator programmes
  • Connect everything in GHL so the ecosystem actually behaves like one ecosystem

By August this is running on autopilot. So is the VSL traffic. So is the post-challenge nurture. You've got a baby coming. The marketing should keep going without you.

A note on the rate

We mentioned on the page that our typical retainer sits between £2,500 and £4,000+VAT. The numbers above are slightly higher than that ceiling.

Here's why: when I shared that range we hadn't seen the full scope. What you've actually asked for is bigger. Two VSL builds, a Brunson-style challenge, £100k+/month ad management, a 170k-list reactivation, ongoing optimisation, and an evergreen-by-August deadline.

We could have quoted lower. We're not going to, because we'd rather be honest about what it takes than over-promise and under-deliver. You've worked with agencies who priced you on turnover. We're pricing you on work. £4,200/£4,850 is what this work properly costs.

Ad spend is separate. Paid directly to the platforms. We don't markup or retain any of it.

What we don't do

So you know what's NOT included:

  • Sales calls and closing (you've got a sales team for that)
  • Customer service or facilitator support (your team handles this)
  • Training your in-house team in copywriting or AI (we'll work alongside Carrie and Dawn and will offer some guidance, but full-on training would be something we'd have to discuss)
  • Coaching delivery, programme delivery, anything client-facing on the certification side

We're a marketing department. We make the marketing work. The business is yours.
